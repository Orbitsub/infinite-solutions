Coolant

P2 — Refined Commodity

Coolant is a Processed (P2) planetary commodity produced by combining Water and Electrolytes in an Advanced Industry Facility. It is a key material in Amarr Fuel Block manufacturing and numerous T2 component blueprints.

Recommended Planet Setup (3 Planets)
Setup Strategy

Coolant requires two distinct planet types — one for each P1 input. The optimal approach uses two extraction planets (each processing their P0 into P1) and one factory planet to combine the P1s into finished Coolant. The ratio is exactly 1 BIF : 1 BIF : 1 AIF for a balanced single-AIF setup.

Balance Rule

Each AIF requires exactly 1 BIF of Water and 1 BIF of Electrolytes. The BIF cycle (30 min, 20 output) produces exactly 40 P1 per hour, which is the precise hourly demand of one AIF. Adding more AIFs means adding the same number of each BIF type to maintain balance.

Tips & Considerations
Extractor Cycle Timing

Shorter extractor cycles (e.g. 1–3 hours) yield higher extraction rates but require more active management. Longer cycles (23–48 hours) are ISK-per-visit efficient but output less total P0. Match your cycle length to how often you can log in.

Buffering P1 Materials

Always include a Storage Facility on Planet 3 to absorb delivery timing differences between your two supply planets. Shipments rarely arrive simultaneously, so a buffer prevents the AIF from stalling.

Extractor Head Placement

Position extractor heads to overlap high-density hotspots in the resource overlay. Reposition heads every cycle — the richest deposits deplete first. Spreading heads across multiple hotspots reduces reposition frequency.

2-Planet Setup (Advanced)

Experienced pilots sometimes combine extraction and factory work on a single planet if it supports both resource types. For Coolant this is generally not possible — Aqueous Liquids and Ionic Solutions are found on different planet types. A 3-planet layout is the standard minimum.

Customs Office Tax

Every export through a Customs Office incurs a tax (POCO or NPC). Factor this into profitability. Exporting P1 or P2 (rather than P0) reduces the number of trips through customs, significantly lowering total tax paid per unit of Coolant.

Amarr Fuel Blocks

Coolant is one of the four PI commodities in Amarr Fuel Blocks (alongside Enriched Uranium, Mechanical Parts & Oxygen). If you manufacture fuel blocks, consider running PI chains for all four — or buy the others and produce only Coolant in-house to reduce supply risk.
